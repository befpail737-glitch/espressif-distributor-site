Troubleshooting Common Wi-Fi Connectivity Issues on ESP32

One of the most common support requests we receive involves Wi-Fi connectivity problems. This guide covers the top three issues our FAEs encounter and how to solve them.

1. The Device Fails to Connect to the AP

This is the most frequent starting problem. Follow these diagnostic steps in order:

  • Check Credentials: The simplest mistake is a typo in the SSID or password. Double-check them.
  • Get the Disconnect Reason: In your ESP-IDF code, register an event handler for `WIFI_EVENT_STA_DISCONNECTED`. The event data will contain a reason code. Codes like `WIFI_REASON_NO_AP_FOUND` or `WIFI_REASON_AUTH_FAIL` will tell you exactly what's wrong.
  • Check Your Antenna: Is the antenna connected properly? If using a PCB trace antenna, was its design (and the required keep-out area) followed exactly as specified in the module datasheet? A poor antenna is a very common hardware issue.

2. High Latency or Packet Loss

If the device connects but the connection is poor, investigate these causes:

  • Signal Strength (RSSI): Print the RSSI value. A value below -75dBm is considered a poor signal and can lead to instability. Try moving the device closer to the router.
  • RF Interference: Other 2.4GHz devices (microwaves, cordless phones, other Wi-Fi networks) can cause interference. Try changing the Wi-Fi channel on your router.
  • Power Supply Issues: Wi-Fi transmission is a power-intensive operation. A weak or unstable power supply can cause "brownouts" where the chip resets or the radio fails. Ensure your power supply can handle current spikes of 300-400mA. Add a large capacitor (e.g., 220uF) close to the module's power pins.

3. Device Disconnects Randomly

This is often the hardest problem to debug. Besides the power supply issues mentioned above, consider:

  • Software Bugs: A memory leak in your application could cause a crash and reset. Monitor free heap memory over time.
  • Router Behavior: Some routers may kick off devices they deem inactive. Ensure you are sending periodic traffic (e.g., a PING or MQTT keep-alive) to maintain the connection.
